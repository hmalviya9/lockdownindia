## India's exposure — the load-bearing data

This is where the user's bull case falls apart. India is **structurally less vulnerable than the regional comparators (Bangladesh, Pakistan) that have already locked down.**

### 5. India is the world's 3rd largest crude importer + 2nd largest LPG importer
- 60% of crude historically from Persian Gulf (Iraq, Saudi, Kuwait, UAE)
- 80–90% of crude from West Asia broadly
- Single biggest source of macro fragility on the war thesis

### 6. BUT — three structural defenses are already deployed

| Defense | Data | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Diversification away from Hormuz** | Per Min. Hardeep Puri (Mar 12, 2026): **70% of India's crude imports already shifted away from Hormuz** | [The Researchers](https://www.theresearchers.us/2026/03/12/india-no-fuel-shortage-crude-imports/) |
| **Strategic crude reserves** | **60 days strategic + 14 days commercial = 74 days cover.** Compare to Bangladesh's near-zero buffer | [The Print](https://theprint.in/economy/indias-strategic-oil-reserves-tell-a-tale-of-structural-constraints-stalled-expansion-heres-why/2889144/), [Organiser](https://organiser.org/2026/03/29/346210/bharat/no-fuel-crisis-in-india-govt-debunks-5-10-days-oil-reserve-claim-assures-60-day-supply-cover/) |
| **Russia surge (non-Hormuz)** | Russia oil to India **+94% MoM in March**, reaching **2.06 mb/d**. US gave India a waiver to scale Russian purchases | [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-03-10/india-snaps-up-30-million-barrels-of-russian-oil-after-us-waiver), [Discovery Alert](https://discoveryalert.com.au/india-procurement-strategy-supply-chain-disruptions-2026/) |

### 7. The LPG vulnerability — real, narrow, but worth tracking

This is the genuine soft spot:
- **LPG strategic storage = ~5 days of demand** (vs 30–90 day international standard)
- India is the world's 2nd largest LPG importer; domestic production 1.158 MT/month vs imports 2.192 MT/month (Jan 2026)
- LPG cylinder delivery delays already reported across multiple cities
- IF the next month's escalation hits LPG specifically (Hormuz LPG flows are concentrated), targeted LPG measures (delivery rationing, household quotas) become quite likely

Source: [VCNow India LPG Crisis 2026](https://learn.vcnow.in/india-lpg-crisis-2026/), [Discovery Alert SPR](https://discoveryalert.com.au/india-energy-security-strategic-reserve-2026/)

- This is the OPPOSITE policy direction from a lockdown — government is signaling demand support, not demand suppression

## Regional precedent — what Bangladesh and Pakistan tell us

This is where the bull case has its strongest evidence — and where it also breaks down on closer reading.

### Bangladesh (CONFIRMED energy lockdown)
- Universities shut nationwide; Eid holidays brought forward
- Nationwide fuel rationing + fuel card system
- Govt offices 9am–4pm only; banks shut by 4pm; malls by 6pm
- Daily attacks on ~3,000 fuel stations; security forces deployed in Dhaka, Chittagong, Sylhet
- **95% energy import dependence** + minimal strategic buffer
- India is supplying diesel TO Bangladesh via the Numaligarh pipeline (180k tonnes/yr) — i.e., India is the regional creditor, not a fellow casualty

Source: [Al Jazeera Bangladesh energy crisis](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/3/9/bangladesh-shuts-universities-limits-fuel-sale-as-iran-war-causes-shortage), [Wikipedia 2026 Bangladesh energy crisis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_Bangladesh_energy_crisis)

### Pakistan (4-day work week, mild)
- Pakistan + Philippines both moved govt offices to 4-day workweeks
- Has not done full lockdown
- Still less restrictive than Bangladesh

### Other rationing/quota countries
Sri Lanka, Myanmar, Bhutan, Slovakia, Slovenia, South Africa, Kenya — quota or restricted fuel sales to prevent hoarding.

**Key observation:** the countries that locked down are all import-dependent at 90%+ with thin reserves. India is at ~85% import dependence but with **74-day strategic cover** AND **70% of imports already off Hormuz** AND **active Russian alternative**. India ≠ Bangladesh on the structural axis that matters.
